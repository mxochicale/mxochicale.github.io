Miguel Xochicale

I am a Research Associate in the Department of Surgical and Interventional Engineering at King's College London where I am contributing to the creation of new hardware, software and algorithms in order to push forward the state-of-the-art of Ultrasound Guided Interventions. In July 2019, I was awarded a PhD in Computer Engineering where one of my achievements is the publication of the first Open Accessible and 100% reproducible PhD thesis since the establishment of the University of Birmingham in 1900.  In four-year PhD, I investigated nonlinear analysis of movement variability in the context of human-robot interaction under the supervision of Prof. Chris Baber (Pervasive and Ubiquitous Computing) and Prof. Martin Russell (Information Engineering).  My background includes electronics and mechatronics, signal processing, artificial intelligence and robotics.

I have passion for Open Science meaning that I release pretty much all my contributions in GitHub @mxochicale. I also tweet and re-tweet about Robotics, AI and Physics @_mxochicale
